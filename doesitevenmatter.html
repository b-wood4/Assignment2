    • Stretching is important for flexibility, range of motion, and injury prevention. When you have better range of motion, you are less likely to be hurt by unexpected movements (like tripping). Stretching also increases blood flow to the muscles, which can help with recovery after a workout. Stretching can also help with posture, and can help to reduce stress.
    • Dynamic stretching involves moving parts of your body and gradually increasing reach or speed, holding the stretch for shorter periods of time. These are typically incorporated into warmups, and should not be done first thing. Warm up a little bit before you stretch to reduce your change of strain or injury.
      Static stretching involves holding a position for a longer amount of time to stretch a muscle or group of muscles. These are typically done after after cool down exercises. Breathing evenly is important, and you don't want to pulse or bounce while static stretching.
